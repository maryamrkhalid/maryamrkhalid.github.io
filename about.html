About Me

About Me

I’m Maryam R. Khalid, currently a Clinical Research Coordinator & Quantitative Biometrics Lab Manager at the Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai, and a M.S. Biostatistics student at Columbia University’s Mailman School of Public Health.

My work focuses on analyzing chronic local field potential (LFP) recordings from deep brain stimulation (DBS) devices to optimize outcomes for treatment-resistant depression (TRD). I integrate neural data with behavioral and clinical outcomes to understand brain rhythms and psychiatric symptom trajectories.
